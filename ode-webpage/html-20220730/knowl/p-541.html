Finally, we will let \(H = 50\text{.}\) In this case, we must solve

\begin{equation*} \frac{dP}{dt} = P\left(1 - \frac{P}{200} \right) - 50 = 0 \end{equation*}

in order to determine any equilibrium solutions. We now obtain a single equilibrium solution at \(P = 100\text{.}\) In fact, \(P = 100\) will be a node.
