In general, suppose that

\begin{align*} \frac{dx}{dt} & = \frac{\partial S}{\partial x}\\ \frac{dy}{dt} & = \frac{\partial S}{\partial y} \end{align*}

is a gradient system. Since

\begin{equation*} \frac{dS}{dt} (x(t), y(t)) = \frac{\partial S}{\partial x} \frac{dx}{dt} + \frac{\partial S}{\partial y} \frac{dy}{dt} = \left( \frac{\partial S}{\partial x} \right)^2 + \left( \frac{\partial S}{\partial y} \right)^2 \geq 0, \end{equation*}

we know that \(S\) increases on every solution to the system except at the critical points of \(S(x(t), y(t))\text{.}\)
