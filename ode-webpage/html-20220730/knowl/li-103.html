Item.

Any first-order linear differential equation

\begin{equation*} y' + p(x) y = g(x) \end{equation*}

can be solved by multiplying each side of the equation by an integrating factor

\begin{equation*} \mu(s) = e^{\int p(x) \, dx}. \end{equation*}

In this case, we get

\begin{equation*} \left( e^{P(x)} y \right)' = e^{P(x)} ( y'(x) + p(x) y(x) ) = e^{P(x)} g(x). \end{equation*}

Integrating both sides,

\begin{equation*} e^{P(x)} y = \int e^{P(x)} g(x) \, dx \end{equation*}

or

\begin{equation*} y = \frac{1}{e^{P(x)} } \int e^{P(x)} g(x) \, dx. \end{equation*}
