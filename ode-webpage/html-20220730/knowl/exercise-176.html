Exercise 3.

If

\begin{equation*} A = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 - i \\ 2 - i & 2 \end{pmatrix} \quad \text{and} \quad B = \begin{pmatrix} 4i & 1 - i \\ 1 + 3i & -2 -i \end{pmatrix}, \end{equation*}

find each of the following.

  1. \(\displaystyle A + B\)

  2. \(\displaystyle 3A - 2B\)

  3. \(\displaystyle AB\)

  4. \(\displaystyle BA\)
