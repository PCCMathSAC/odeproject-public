Exercise 2.

If

\begin{equation*} A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & -2 \\ 3 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \quad \text{and} \quad B = \begin{pmatrix} 4 & 1 \\ -1 & -2 \end{pmatrix}, \end{equation*}

find each of the following.

  1. \(\displaystyle A + B\)

  2. \(\displaystyle 2A - 3B\)

  3. \(\displaystyle AB\)

  4. \(\displaystyle BA\)

  5. \(\displaystyle A^{-1}\)

  6. \(\displaystyle B^{-1}\)
